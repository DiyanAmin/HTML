Below are the BEST tourist places in India

Taj Mahal


The Taj Mahal is a world-renowned symbol of love and Mughal architectural brilliance, built in Agra, India, between 1631 and 1653 by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.
The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum complex that stands on the southern bank of the Yamuna River in Agra, Uttar Pradesh. It is considered the finest example of Mughal architecture, blending Indian, Persian, and Islamic styles into a harmonious masterpiece. Rising to a height of about 73 meters (240 feet), the monument is set within a vast garden and flanked by a mosque and guest house, all enclosed by red sandstone walls. Its marble surfaces change hues with the shifting daylight, creating an ethereal effect that has captivated visitors for centuries.
Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of tourists annually, who come to witness its beauty and the timeless story of devotion it represents.

Red Fort


The Red Fort, located in Delhi, is one of India’s most iconic monuments and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1639, it served as the main residence of the Mughal rulers for nearly 200 years. Constructed with striking red sandstone, the fort showcases a blend of Persian, Timurid, and Indian architectural styles. Its massive walls, intricate carvings, and beautiful gardens reflect the grandeur of the Mughal era. The Red Fort also holds great historical significance, as it is the site where India’s Prime Minister hoists the national flag every Independence Day, symbolizing the nation’s pride and unity.
